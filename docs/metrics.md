# ActionOS Metrics System

## Overview

ActionOS measures **real progress**, not vanity metrics. Our system captures both external impact and internal alignment through a unique combination of metrics that reveal true value creation, momentum, and directionality.

---

## Primary Metrics

### 1. IPP — Impact Per Person (Output Reality Index)

**Definition**: Measures how much real-world, externally verifiable impact your completed Step-1 actions produce on actual humans, systems, or outcomes.

**Formula**: 
```
IPP = Magnitude × Reach × Depth
```

**Components**:
- **Magnitude** (1-10): Size of the change created
- **Reach** (1-∞): Number of people affected  
- **Depth** (0.1-3.0): Surface-level (0.1) to life-changing (3.0)

**What Counts as Impact**:
Something that changes another person's:
- Decision-making ability
- Clarity or understanding
- Convenience or efficiency
- Stress level or mental state
- Speed of execution
- Access to resources/information
- Cost or financial state
- Productivity or output
- Revenue or business metrics
- Confidence or capability
- Life outcomes or trajectory

**Time Periods Tracked**:
- Daily IPP
- 7-day IPP
- 30-day IPP
- All-time IPP
- IPP per Insight (diagnostic)

**Why It Matters**: Only impact compounds. Activities, hours, and task completion are vanity metrics. IPP captures external value creation—the root of promotions, career progression, trust, reputation, business growth, and personal transformation.

**Examples**:
- IPP 0: Reorganized your notes (busy, no external change)
- IPP 15: Sent a clear email that saved 3 people 2 hours each
- IPP 240: Created a process that helped 20 team members work 30% faster
- IPP 5000: Launched a feature used by 1000 customers daily

---

### 2. BUT — Barakah Per Unit Time (Blessed Efficiency Index)

**Definition**: Measures how much goodness, smoothness, ease, clarity, and unexpectedly positive outcomes flow from your actions relative to time invested.

**Formula**:
```
BUT = (Positive Outcomes / Time Invested) × (Alignment + Momentum + Friction) Multipliers
```

**Components Measured**:
- Clarity gained vs time spent
- Doors opened vs effort invested
- Unexpected help or opportunities
- Reduced friction or obstacles
- Speed of results vs expectations
- Distraction resistance
- Mental ease and flow state
- Alignment with values/faith/identity

**Multipliers**:
- **Alignment Factor** (0.5-2.0): How well action aligns with core values
- **Momentum Factor** (0.5-2.0): How much action builds on existing progress  
- **Friction Factor** (-1.0-1.0): Obstacles encountered (negative) or removed (positive)

**Why It Matters**: Linear effort → linear output. Barakah → multiplicative output. BUT captures qualitative acceleration—the dimension of progress that feels "guided," "aligned," or "flow-state amplified."

**High BUT Indicators**:
- Less burnout
- Higher purpose alignment
- Fewer mistakes
- Better timing
- Things go smoother than expected

**Low BUT Indicators**:
- Forcing things
- Misalignment with values
- Wasted time
- Activities that "technically count" but drain energy

**Relationship to IPP**:
- **BUT** = inner progress (alignment, ease, momentum)
- **IPP** = outer progress (external impact, value creation)
- **Together** = full-spectrum progress

## Secondary Metrics

### 3. Insight Conversion Rate (ICR)

**Definition**: Percentage of insights that turn into Step-1 actions.

**Formula**:
```
ICR = (Insights with Completed Step-1s / Total Insights) × 100
```

**Interpretation**:
- **High ICR (>70%)**: User is applying thought into reality, bias toward action
- **Medium ICR (40-70%)**: Healthy balance of reflection and execution
- **Low ICR (<40%)**: Overthinking, analysis paralysis, no execution

---

### 4. Step-1 Success Rate (S1SR)

**Definition**: Percentage of Step-1 actions that result in measurable IPP or BUT.

**Formula**:
```
S1SR = (Step-1s with IPP>0 or BUT>baseline / Total Completed Step-1s) × 100
```

**Interpretation**:
- **High S1SR (>80%)**: User is choosing the right Step-1s, high execution quality
- **Medium S1SR (50-80%)**: Good but room for better prioritization
- **Low S1SR (<50%)**: Poor Step-1 selection, misdirected effort

**Why It Matters**: Shows whether user is choosing *the right* actions, not just any actions.

---

### 5. Reality Shift Index (RSI)

**Definition**: Meta-metric combining IPP and BUT trends to answer: "Is my life moving in the direction I want?"

**Formula**:
```
RSI = (IPP Trend × 0.6) + (BUT Trend × 0.4)
Trend = (Current Period - Previous Period) / Previous Period
```

**Interpretation**:
- **RSI > 0.2**: Strong positive momentum, compounding progress
- **RSI 0-0.2**: Steady progress, maintain course
- **RSI -0.2-0**: Stagnation or slight decline, adjust strategy
- **RSI < -0.2**: Negative trajectory, urgent course correction needed

**Why It Matters**: This is what founders and executives care about—directional certainty, not just productivity.

---

### 6. Time Allocation Accuracy (TAA)

**Definition**: How accurately the user predicts time needed vs actual time spent.

**Formula**:
```
TAA = 1 - (|Estimated Time - Actual Time| / Estimated Time)
Average TAA = Mean of all Step-1 TAA scores
```

**Interpretation**:
- **TAA > 0.8**: Excellent planning precision, realistic expectations
- **TAA 0.6-0.8**: Good estimation, minor adjustments needed
- **TAA < 0.6**: Poor estimation, indicates burnout risk or low executive function

---

### 7. High-Leverage Action Density (HLAD)

**Definition**: Percentage of actions that fall in the top 20% of leverage (Pareto principle).

**Formula**:
```
HLAD = (Number of High-Leverage Step-1s / Total Step-1s) × 100
High-Leverage = IPP > 80th percentile of user's historical IPP
```

**Interpretation**:
- **HLAD > 30%**: Exceptional leverage discipline, focused on what matters
- **HLAD 15-30%**: Good leverage awareness, some noise
- **HLAD < 15%**: Too much low-impact activity, diluted focus

## Metric Relationships

### The Progress Triangle
```
        IPP (External Impact)
           /\
          /  \
         /    \
        /      \
       /________\
    BUT         RSI
 (Internal)  (Direction)
```

### Diagnostic Combinations

**High IPP + High BUT**: Optimal state—aligned, impactful, flowing
**High IPP + Low BUT**: Grinding—results but burnout risk
**Low IPP + High BUT**: Comfortable but ineffective—feels good, no impact
**Low IPP + Low BUT**: Crisis—misaligned and ineffective

**High ICR + Low S1SR**: Action bias without quality—doing wrong things fast
**Low ICR + High S1SR**: Overthinking but quality when executing

**Rising RSI + High HLAD**: Compounding success—reinforcing positive trajectory
**Falling RSI + Low HLAD**: Spiral risk—scattered effort, declining results

## Why This System Captures True Progress

### What Traditional Apps Measure
❌ **Streaks** (arbitrary consistency)
❌ **Hours** (time spent, not value created)
❌ **Tasks** (activity, not outcomes)
❌ **Projects** (intentions, not results)
❌ **Habits** (inputs, not outputs)

### What ActionOS Measures
✅ **Outcomes** (external change via IPP)
✅ **Value Produced** (impact per action)
✅ **Alignment** (internal coherence via BUT)
✅ **Compounding Direction** (trajectory via RSI)
✅ **Reality Shifts** (actual life changes)
✅ **Conversion Quality** (thinking → doing via ICR)
✅ **Leverage Discipline** (vital few via HLAD)

**Old**: How many hours did you work?
**New**: How much value did you create per hour?

**Old**: Are you consistent?
